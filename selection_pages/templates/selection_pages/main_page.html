LipReading

Lipreading is a primary mode of communication for people with hearing loss. India is home to approximately 63 million people of the Deaf and Hard of Hearing Community (DHH). Despite these staggering stats, online lipreading training resources are scarce and available for only a few languages. However, hosting new lipreading training platforms is an extensive ordeal that can take months of manual effort.

Existing Lipreading training platforms

Currently, three popular lipreading training platforms exist. Unfortunately, these platforms are only available in English, excluding more than 80% of potential lipreaders across the globe.

Our solutions

We propose a fully-automated approach to building large-scale lipreading training platforms. Our approach enables any language, any accent, and unlimited vocabulary on any identity! We envision a lipreading MOOCs platform to enable millions of people with hearing loss across the globe.

Why lipreading?

Research shows that it is extremely difficult for the DHH community to get an education as they cannot obtain basic skills that the “hearing” population would at a young age.

Pipeline Overview

Data collection module

Random videos are first collected from various online sources such as YouTube. Random videos introduce real-world variations a lipreader encounters in real life, such as variations in the head movement of the speaker, distance of the speaker from the camera, speaker’s complexion, and lip structure.

Preprocessing module

The videos are pre-processed with a face-detection model to detect valid videos. Valid videos are single-identity front-facing talking head videos with little to no pose changes.

TTS generation module

Speech utterances are generated using TTS models on large vocabulary in any language/accent curated automatically from online sources.

Audio-Video Alignment module

Randomly select a pair of driving speech and a face video. To generate lip-synced videos, the video and speech utterance lengths are matched by aligning them and then padding the speech utterance with silence. Lip-landmark detection-based computer-vision techniques detect mouth movements in the face videos; the audio is aligned on the detected video region, and silences are added around the speech.

Data generation using talking head generation module

The aligned speech utterance and the face video are passed through Wav2Lip. Wav2Lip modifies the lip movements in the original video and preserves the original head movements, background, and camera variations, thus creating realistic-looking synthetic videos in the wild.

Quiz module

The synthetically generated videos are then used to backfill the backend of predefined lipreading training quizzes on the fly introducing novel videos with real-world variations in the speaker’s lip movements for any language/accent.
